Why Your Brain Won't Stop Thinking

Overthinking is not simply thinking too much. It is a cognitive loop known as rumination, where the brain repeatedly analyzes the same thoughts without reaching resolution. Neuroscience research shows that rumination activates the default mode network, the system responsible for self-referential thinking. When this system becomes overactive, the mind becomes trapped replaying conversations, imagining worst-case scenarios, and analyzing decisions endlessly.
